0.2 REVISION SYSTEM

The EuroPilot Center Operations Manual and its amendments are proposed, drafted, issued, and after a BCAA spot check, accepted and implemented by the Head of Training.

The Compliance Monitoring Manager ensures that the content of the Training Manual complies with all applicable regulations and requirements.

Any amendment proposal by the Head of Training shall be executed by sending an email to the Compliance Monitoring Manager and the Accountable Manager. The email shall include the revised draft of the applicable manual, section or paragraph, and the reason(s) for the amendment.

Upon verification by the Compliance Monitoring Manager, the amendment will be submitted to the BCAA.

All amendments shall be accompanied by an updated List of Effective Parts, identification of changes by a vertical bar placed in front of the amended text, and a Highlight of Changes paragraph published as part of this manual.

Digital, password-protected PDF versions of the Operations Manual may be distributed and marked as “Uncontrolled Copy”. These files are protected against printing.

Note: A Temporary Revision Procedure is not applicable within EuroPilot Center B/ATO-017.

0.6 DEFINITIONS AND TERMS

Aerobatic flight: Intentional manoeuvres involving abrupt attitude changes not required for normal flight or instruction.
Aircraft: Any machine that can derive support in the atmosphere from the reactions of the air.
Flight time: Total time from the moment an aircraft first moves for the purpose of taking off until the moment it finally comes to rest at the end of the flight.
Instrument flight time: Time during which a pilot controls an aircraft solely by reference to instruments.
Pilot-in-Command (PIC): The pilot designated as responsible for the safe conduct of the flight.
Student Pilot-in-Command (SPIC): A student pilot acting as pilot-in-command under the supervision of a flight instructor.
