14. CONTRACTED TRAINING

14.1 General

In accordance with ORA.GEN.205, contracted activities include all activities within EuroPilot Center’s ATO scope of approval that are performed by another organization either:

• itself certified to carry out such activity; or
• working under the EuroPilot Center’s approval.

EuroPilot Center is responsible to ensure that all contracted activities are subject to hazard identification and risk management as required by ORA.GEN.200(a)(3) and to compliance monitoring as required by ORA.GEN.200(a)(6).



14.1.1 Organizations certified

A written agreement should exist between the organization and the contracted organization clearly defining the contracted activities and the applicable requirements.

A copy of this agreement must be sent to the BCAA prior to the start of the contracted activities.

EuroPilot Center’s compliance monitoring system will at least check that the approval of the contractor effectively covers the contracted activities and that it is still valid.



14.1.2 Organizations not certified

When EuroPilot Center contracts any part of its activity to an organization that is not itself certified in accordance with this Part to carry out such activity, the contracted organization shall work under the approval of EuroPilot Center.

A written agreement should exist between the organization and the contracted organization clearly defining the contracted activities and the applicable requirements.

A copy of this agreement must be sent to the BCAA prior to the start of the contracted activities.

In addition, EuroPilot Center shall ensure that the BCAA is given access to the contracted organization, to determine continued compliance with the applicable requirements.



14.2 Procedure to establish a contracted activity

The requirements as mentioned under the EuroPilot Center OMM must be met prior to the start of the contracted activities:

• Part 2, Compliance Monitoring Management, Chapter 8 – Contractor / Supplier Management; and
• Part 3, Safety Management Manual, Chapter 7 – Contracted Activities.

The Accountable Manager is responsible to evaluate the financial and safety aspects of the cooperation and send a “Change Request” to the Safety Manager AND Compliance Monitoring Manager in order to trigger a “Management of Change” procedure.

We refer to the procedure in OMM Part 3 – Chapter 5.9 Management of Change.

16. CHANGES NOT REQUIRING PRIOR APPROVAL OF THE BCAA

In accordance with ORA.GEN.130(c), the following changes do not require prior approval by the BCAA:

• changes in Theoretical Knowledge Personnel;
• changes in Flight Instruction Personnel;
• adding or removing aircraft.

The Accountable Manager is authorized to hire new training staff and add or remove aircraft to the training fleet as required by the operational needs of the organization.

We refer to OM Part 4 – Chapter 1, Responsibilities.

For every change, the Accountable Manager is responsible to send a “Change Request” to the Safety Manager AND Compliance Monitoring Manager in order to trigger a “Management of Change” procedure.

We refer to the procedure in OMM Part 3 – Chapter 5.9 Management of Change.



16.1 Hiring new Theoretical Knowledge Instructors (TKI’s)

Every new Theoretical Knowledge Instructor shall in accordance with AMC1 ORA.ATO.110(c) have a practical background in aviation in the areas relevant for the training provided and have undergone a course of training in instructional techniques, or have previous experience in giving theoretical knowledge instruction and an appropriate theoretical background in the subject on which the candidate will provide theoretical knowledge instruction.

The defined hiring procedure shall be followed.

• Step 1 – The Accountable Manager sends a “Change Request” to the Safety Manager AND Compliance Monitoring Manager via e-mail. The header of the e-mail must contain: “Change Request: New TKI + FIRST NAME, LAST NAME” and the content of the e-mail should outline the details of the proposed changes, including the Resume of the applicant and additional relevant data.

• Step 2 – Upon receiving this e-mail, the Safety Manager creates a new “Change Request Project” in Pipedrive and saves it under Phase 1 of the Project Pipeline in Pipedrive.

• Step 3 – The Safety Manager moves on to Phase 2 of the Project Pipeline in Pipedrive and executes the “Management of Change” procedure as described in the OMM Part 3.

• Step 4 – The Safety Manager drafts his/her findings in the Project Notes and uploads any additional required PDF documents (if required) in the project.

• Step 5 – the Safety Manager moves on to Phase 3 of the Project Pipeline and creates an “Assessment Task/Event Checklist” (this process is monitored by the CMM) containing:
	1.	Verification of relevant experience by the Safety Manager + Deadline.
	2.	Uploading ID/Passport in the Project.
	3.	Scheduling and executing a Psychological Assessment: PELICAN Test online.
	4.	Motivational Interview (in-person or online) by: AM, SM, HT or Chief TKI.
	5.	Scheduling/executing a supervised Test Lecture, by the HT or Chief TKI.
	6.	Scheduling/execution “Decision Meeting” with AM, CMM, HT and Chief TKI.
	7.	Final Verification of the CMM performed.

• Step 6 – The Safety Manager records the minutes of the “Decision Meeting” in the Project Notes and sends an e-mail to the AM, CMM, HT and Chief TKI which must include the final decision on hiring the applicant or not.

Option A - After a positive evaluation of steps 1 to 6 above:

• Step 7a - The Safety Manager creates a “Hiring Task/Event Checklist” containing:
	1.	Sending an e-mail to applicant notifying about the decision taken.
	2.	Sending out the Theoretical Knowledge Instructor Contract via Docusign.
	3.	Scheduling the require standardization training.
	4.	Sending an e-mail to the applicant outlining the standardization training items.
	5.	Adding the applicant to the Theoretical Knowledge Instructor Annex.
	6.	The Accountable Manager notifies the BCAA of the upcoming change via e-mail, at least 10 days before the new Theoretical Knowledge Instructor will start his/her duties. He/she shall attach the updated Theoretical knowledge Instructor Annex.
	7.	Instructor Data saved in E-gle + FlightCircle by the Operations Manager.
	8.	Final Verification by the CMM performed.

Option B - After a negative evaluation of steps 1 to 6 above:

• Step 7b - The Safety Manager creates an “End of Project Checklist” containing:
	1.	Sending an e-mail to applicant notifying about the decision taken.
	2.	Sending an e-mail to the AM, CMM, HT and Chief TKI about the outcome.

• Step 8 - The Compliance Monitoring Manager verifies if all steps in the process above are executed as described and moves the Project to Stage 4 of the Project Pipeline: End.



16.2 Hiring new Flight Instructors (FI(A), CRI, IRI, MCCI’s)

Every new Flight Instructor shall in accordance with ORA.ATO.110(d) hold the qualifications required by Part-FCL for the type of training that the candidate will provide on an aircraft and/or FSTD.

The defined hiring procedure shall be followed.

• Step 1 – The Accountable Manager sends a “Change Request” to the Safety Manager AND Compliance Monitoring Manager via e-mail. The header of the e-mail must contain: “Change Request: New FI/CRI/IRI/MCCI + FIRST NAME, LAST NAME” and the content of the e-mail should outline the details of the proposed changes, including the Resume of the applicant and additional relevant data (e.g. Medical, License, Logbook Scan, Additional Certificates).

• Step 2 – Upon receiving this e-mail, the Safety Manager creates a new “Change Request Project” in Pipedrive and saves it under Phase 1 of the Project Pipeline in Pipedrive.

• Step 3 – The Safety Manager moves on to Phase 2 of the Project Pipeline in Pipedrive and executes the “Management of Change” procedure as described in the OMM Part 3.

• Step 4 – The Safety Manager drafts his/her findings in the Project Notes and uploads any additional required PDF documents (if required) in the project.

• Step 5 – the Safety Manager moves on to Phase 3 of the Project Pipeline and creates an “Assessment Task/Event Checklist” (this process is monitored by the CMM) containing:
	1.	Verification of relevant experience by the Safety Manager + Deadline.
	2.	Uploading: ID/Passport, Medical Class, Pilot License and the last page of the applicant’s Logbook in the Project.
	3.	Scheduling and executing a Psychological Assessment: PELICAN Test online.
	4.	Motivational Interview (in-person or online) by: AM, HT or CFI.
	5.	Scheduling/executing a 1 hour assessment by the HT or his/her deputy or CFI on the FSTD in order to verify the instructional skills of the applicant.
	6.	Scheduling/execution of a “Decision Meeting” with the AM, CMM, HT and CFI.
	7.	Final Verification of the CMM performed.

• Step 6 – The Safety Manager records the minutes of the “Decision Meeting” in the Project Notes and sends an e-mail to the AM, CMM, HT and CFI which must include the final decision on hiring the applicant or not.

16.3 Adding / Removing Aircraft

A new training aircraft shall fulfill the requirements in accordance with ORA.ATO.135 and AMC1 ORA.ATO.135.

The following procedure applies:

• Step 1 – The Accountable Manager sends a “Change Request” to the Safety Manager AND Compliance Monitoring Manager via e-mail. The header of the e-mail must contain: “Change Request: New Aircraft + REGISTRATION” and the content of the e-mail should outline the details of the proposed changes, including the relevant data (e.g. Aircraft Documents).

• Step 2 – Upon receiving this e-mail, the Safety Manager creates a new “Change Request Project” in Pipedrive and saves it under Phase 1 of the Project Pipeline in Pipedrive.

• Step 3 – The Safety Manager moves on to Phase 2 of the Project Pipeline in Pipedrive and executes the “Management of Change” procedure as described in the OMM Part 3.

As a guideline, the following items should at least be taken into consideration by the SM:
	•	Is the new aircraft suitable for the home and alternate bases (are there any limitations)?
	•	Is the development of new SOP’s required?
	•	Is an update of the relevant parts of the OM and Training Manuals required?
	•	Is a refresher/standardization training required for Flight Instructors needed?
	•	Is there maintenance available?

• Step 4 – The Safety Manager drafts his/her findings in the Project Notes and uploads any additional required PDF documents (if required) in the project.

• Step 5 – the Safety Manager moves on to Phase 3 of the Project Pipeline and creates an “Assessment Task/Event Checklist” (this process is monitored by the CMM) containing:
	1.	Management of Change Procedure Safety Item(s) + Mitigation Actions added by the Safety Manager.
	2.	Certificate of Registration checked/uploaded (Pipedrive) by the SM.
	3.	Certificate of Airworthiness checked/uploaded (Pipedrive) by the SM.
	4.	Valid Airworthiness Review Certificate checked/uploaded (Pipedrive) by the SM.
	5.	Noise Certificate checked/uploaded (Pipedrive) by the SM.
	6.	Aircraft Radio Station License checked/uploaded (Pipedrive) by the SM.
	7.	Last page of the Journey Log checked/uploaded (Pipedrive) by the SM.
	8.	MEL/CDL (if applicable) checked/uploaded (Pipedrive) by the SM.
	9.	Last revision of POH/checklist checked/uploaded (Pipedrive) by the SM.
	10.	Maintenance (eg. Last 100h/Annual) checked/uploaded (Pipedrive) by the SM.
	11.	Third Party Liability Insurance checked/uploaded (Pipedrive) by the SM.
	12.	Copy of CAMO Contract checked/uploaded (Pipedrive) by the SM.
	13.	Verification of Squawk Items: checked/uploaded (Pipedrive) by the SM.
	14.	Final Verification of the CMM performed.

• Step 6 – The Safety Manager records possible findings in the Project Notes and sends an e-mail to the AM, CMM, and HT outlining these findings.

A notification shall be sent by the Accountable Manager via e-mail at least 10 working days before the date of the proposed change.

• The e-mail address to be used: BCAA.ATO@mobilit.fgov.be

The subject of the e-mail to be sent by the Accountable Manager shall be as follows:
